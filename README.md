Does going to university in a different country affect mental health?

I completed this project by analysing students’ mental health to investigate whether studying in a different country affected well-being. 
The project was based on a 2018 survey conducted by a Japanese international university, which was published the following year after 
receiving approval from several ethical and regulatory boards. The original study concluded that international students were at a higher 
risk of experiencing mental health difficulties compared to the general population. It also found that social connectedness (a sense of 
belonging to a social group) and acculturative stress (stress experienced while adapting to a new culture) were significant predictors of 
depression.

To examine whether I would reach a similar conclusion, I explored the students dataset using PostgreSQL. I analysed the data to 
compare the mental health indicators of international and domestic students, focusing on levels of depression, social connectedness, and 
acculturative stress.Additionally, I investigated whether the length of stay in the host country was a contributing factor to mental health 
outcomes. By querying, filtering, and aggregating the dataset in PostgreSQL, I assessed patterns and relationships to determine whether 
my findings aligned with the conclusions of the original study.
